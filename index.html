"The most important question is what is the right question."

Most brand problems exist because the sender doesn't know what they want to convey. Before saying anything to the world about a brand, I make sure the brand knows what it's saying — and why. That's not strategy. That's just asking the questions nobody else bothered to ask.

The Work

CASE STUDIES

Given a brand doing ₹3L/month with an identity crisis. Fixed the basics first — helped them figure out who they are, sharpened the menu to match that story, priced it to reflect it. Ran performance marketing to bring traffic, used their own data to improve conversion and retention. Turned viewers into first-timers, first-timers into regulars. Grew to ₹18L by month 7, sustainably, with healthy margins.

The insight was personal — I'd order food at work and never know if I could actually eat it at my desk without making a mess. Nobody was solving for certainty. My SKU contribution: rice bowls that worked across cuisines (north Indian, south Indian, Andhra, Chinese), meal combos with a cutlery kit, portions adjusted so the pricing made sense. Items that travelled well, looked intentional, solved "no clutter while eating." Weekday volumes moved. Rolled out nationally.

People associate specialised-sounding brands with higher quality — even if it comes from the same kitchen. Like thinking a comedian can't do serious roles. A multicuisine restaurant happy at breakeven had no reason for anyone to choose them. Split into focused cloud kitchens. The Asian kitchen alone in its first month outperformed the full restaurant's last month. Smaller menus helped too — someone looking for noodles doesn't want to scroll past pasta first.

Restaurant owners don't buy ad packages. They buy a future they can see themselves in. Stopped pitching impressions, started pitching 9-month roadmaps — location, cuisine fit, pricing, SKU strategy backed by data. For one chain planning 5 outlets in 9 months, sat in menu sampling and packaging reviews. Gave them a picture of who they'd be. They invested. Closed ₹20–30L monthly while maintaining a 4.3× ROI — 50% above the 2.8× city average.

Marketing isn't just about attracting customers — it's about understanding what they actually need. With farmers who had every reason to say no, we listened first. Heard every problem, big or small. Replaced jargon with examples from their own lives. Designed a payment model where costs were deducted from milk procurement — removed upfront risk entirely. First farmer said yes, their story became the pitch for the next village. Patience and listening, not campaigns.
